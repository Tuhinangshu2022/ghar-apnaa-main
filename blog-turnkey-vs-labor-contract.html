Turnkey vs Labor Contract: Which is Best for Your Home Construction?

June 2, 2026 • 6 min read

When you decide to build a house, one of the first major decisions is determining how you will manage the construction. The two most common paths are signing a Turnkey Contract or a Labor Contract. Here is a breakdown to help you decide.

1. The Turnkey Contract (Material + Labor)

In a turnkey contract, the builder or contractor takes full responsibility for the entire project from start to finish. They procure the materials, manage the workforce, oversee the daily progress, and deliver the finished house ("turn the key").

  • Pros: Tremendous peace of mind. You don't have to deal with vendors, material shortages, or daily site management. It is highly time-efficient.
  • Cons: It is generally more expensive. If you don't use a vetted professional, there is a risk of them using sub-standard materials to widen their profit margin.

2. The Labor Contract

In a labor contract, you only pay the contractor for the workforce. As the homeowner, you are entirely responsible for purchasing, transporting, and managing the inventory of all construction materials (cement, steel, bricks, etc.).

  • Pros: You have absolute control over the quality of materials used. It can be more cost-effective if you have good negotiation skills with vendors.
  • Cons: It requires a massive time commitment. You must visit the site daily, manage inventory to prevent theft, and coordinate delivery logistics. Delays in material delivery stall the entire project.

Conclusion

If you have a full-time job and cannot visit the site daily, a Turnkey contract with a verified professional from a trusted platform like Ghar Apnaa is the safest route. However, if you have the time, energy, and industry knowledge, a Labor contract gives you granular control over your build.
